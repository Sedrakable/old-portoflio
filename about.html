Backstory
Hello, my name is Sedrak Nadzharyan, I am 21 years old, and I am trying to be a professional web developer. Why? Well, I will need to explain how I got to my current situation.
Yearly life
For as long as I can remember, I loved arts and crafts, as well as solving puzzles. Starting school at age 6, I excelled in mathematics and took extracurricular Arts courses, where I learned the basics of drawing, painting, and sculpting. I continued in that trajectory for three years until I moved to Canada. At that point, I continued my schooling in Canada, but since I didn't know the languages at the time, I could not take extra art classes. So I took the matter into my own hands and started doing Origami at home. It started with a paper plane and turned into complicated tessellations in a matter of 2 years. I was hooked. At this point, my family had to go back to Russia. For the next two years, I have mostly focused on studying. Then, yes again, I permanently moved back to Canada. I was lacking in the language department from all the moving, but my Math skills from Russia propelled me, and I finished my french high school (Collège français) faster than I anticipated. For all those years, I was still doing small projects on the side.
College
After high-school, I entered Vanier College, where I learned so much and changed a lot as a person. Knowing that I love mathematics and designing things, I decided to choose Computer Science and Mathematics as my course. At this point, I knew nothing about programming, but I was ready to learn. In 4 semesters, I learned Java, Java FX, and CSS. And I loved that experience. Additionally, for my complimentary courses, I chose a Painting and a Drawing course. That was probably one of the best decisions of my life. I loved every second of that experience, and those skills now help me create icons, logos, or any sort of asset for my programming projects.
Work
After I got my degree in Computer-science and Mathematics, I wasn't sure what I wanted to pursue, so I decided to find a job for the time being. I joined Keywords Studios, a Video Game Testing Company as an FQA tester, I worked there for about a year. From there I learned different aspects of games and how to test them, how to work with others (as a team), learned to report issues, learned to communicate with developers, help recruits, and in general get tasks done as fast as possible. Additionally, I worked on Web (software) testing for one of my projects. In a year got to an Expert Tester and now I am ready to pursue a developer job.
