Scupting Projects

Sculpting is more or less a new art form for me. There is for sure more to come in this section.

Red Hood Helmet

This is a recreation of a helmet of a fictional character called the Red Hood, for Holloween. Using kids foam puzzles and a glue-gun, I cut-out individual pieces and put all together to create the shape. Then I cleaned up the edges and spray painted the whole mask red. I had to recreate the mast 3 times because the first time the mask came out too small for my head, the second time it came out too big.
Size: Normal head-shape
Date: 2015

Tower Bridge base

This is a popsicle stick recreation of the London Tower Bridge base with a moving deck. This was made as a school project, but I intend to recreate or to finish the whole bridge.
Size: 18" x 24"
Date: 2017

Red Crumpled Paper

The painting was a recreation of a piece of paper that I've crumpled. This painting made me improve my stroke precision, because of the hard edges. Also, the important transitions from light to shadow made me more meticulous when blending colors. The piece required a lot of retouching, but I think it turned out good.
Size: 18" x 24"
Date: 2017
